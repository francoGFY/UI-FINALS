GARLIC
Garlic is a pungent, edible bulb from the plant Allium sativum. Its key medicinal component is allicin, which is formed when cloves are crushed or chopped.
What it is used for?
  • Cardiovascular Health: Clinical studies suggest it can slightly lower blood pressure (hypertension) and improve cholesterol levels (specifically reducing LDL “bad” cholesterol).
  • Immune Support: Traditionally used to shorten the duration of colds and flu due to its perceived antimicrobial and anti-bacterial properties.
  • Blood Sugar Regulation: May help stabilize blood sugar levels in people with type 2 diabetes.
Where to find it in the Philippines
  • Markets: Fresh local or imported garlic is in every wet and dry market and supermarket.
  • Drugstores/Online: Look for “Aged Garlic Extract” (like Kyolic) or specific brands of garlic oil softgels at Watsons, Mercury Drug, Shopee, Lazada.
  • Health Stores: Health Options stocks various high-quality garlic supplements.
How to use it properly?
  • Preparation: For maximum medicinal benefit, crush or chop raw garlic and let it sit for 10–15 minutes before cooking, or consume it raw.
  • Dosage (Aged Garlic Extract): Many studies use a standard aged garlic extract dosage of 600mg to 1,200mg daily (e.g., 200mg per meal).
  • Cooking: Cooking for too long can reduce its medicinal compounds. Add towards the end of cooking for flavor and minimal heat exposure.
  • The Form Matters: Available as standardized extracts, aged garlic, garlic oil capsules, or raw. Standardized extract is most reliable.
When you should use it
  • Use it if you seek to supplement cardiac health, support a healthy immune response, or manage mild cholesterol issues.
  • DON’T use it if: You are on blood-thinning medications (it has antiplatelet effects), you have a bleeding disorder, or you have severe digestive issues (may cause irritation).
